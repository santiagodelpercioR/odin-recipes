Sorrentinos Recipe

Lorem ipsum dolor

Originally filled with ham and cheese, sorrentinos—a round stuffed pasta two to three times the size of a typical ravioli—can be found all over Argentina.

In Buenos Aires, people are as loyal to their choice pasta shop as they are to their favorite football team

Ingredients

  • 140g (3/4 cup; 5 ounces) semolina, plus more for dusting
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons Diamond Crystal kosher salt; for table or fine sea salt, use half as much by volume
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 25ml (1 tablespoon plus 2 teaspoons) room-temperature water
  • 14 ounces (400g) king oyster mushrooms, cut into 1/4-inch cubes
  • 1/2 cup (60ml) extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon grated nutmeg

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine semolina and salt. Form a well in the center about 4 inches wide.
  2. Unwrap dough and set on a semolina-floured work surface. Using a semolina-floured rolling pin, flatten dough into an oblong shape about 1/2 inch thick.
  3. Narrow the setting by 1 notch and pass dough through, then run it through again.
  4. Remove dough from bag, unfold, and cut in half. Return one dough sheet to zipper-lock bag.
  5. While the dough is resting in Step 1, make the filling.
  6. Heat a 12-inch cast iron or stainless-steel skillet over medium-high heat until almost smoking.
  7. Stir in leeks along with a pinch of salt, and cook, stirring occasionally, until leeks are translucent, about 8 minutes.
  8. Dust a rimmed baking sheet with a thin coating of semolina flour and set aside.
  9. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  10. Add sorrentinos to boiling water.
